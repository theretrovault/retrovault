# Retro Software Suite — Brand and Naming System

## Purpose

This document proposes naming logic for the future retro-focused software suite and its family of apps.

The goal is to avoid random names and instead create a brand system that feels coherent, extensible, and emotionally distinctive.

---

## Desired brand qualities

The suite should feel:
- retro but not kitschy
- emotional but not sentimental mush
- cool but not try-hard
- modular but not corporate
- slightly mythic
- slightly cyberpunk
- archival, tactile, and alive

Good brand territory includes:
- memory
- time
- signal
- preservation
- glow
- artifacts
- movement through layers

---

## Naming approaches

## Approach A: One master suite brand + app sub-brands

Example:
- **Timekeeper** (master brand)
- RetroVault
- TapeDeck
- Reverse Matrix
- Scanline
- CartMarket
- Arcade
- SaveState
- ShowRunner

### Pros
- strongest individuality per app
- easiest to make each app feel like a distinct experience
- best if some apps become their own products with different audiences

### Cons
- slightly less obvious family relationship at first glance

### Recommendation
This is the strongest overall approach.

---

## Approach B: Master brand prefix system

Example:
- **Timekeeper Vault**
- **Timekeeper TapeDeck**
- **Timekeeper Scanline**
- **Timekeeper Matrix**

### Pros
- strong family cohesion
- easy to understand relationship
- useful in formal branding and site architecture

### Cons
- can get clunky
- risks weakening the standalone character of the best app names

### Recommendation
Good as a secondary presentation layer, not necessarily the user-facing primary naming layer.

---

## Approach C: Shared thematic naming, no explicit parent in every title

Example family:
- RetroVault
- TapeDeck
- Scanline
- SaveState
- Ghost Signal
- Afterglow
- Memory Circuit

### Pros
- stylish and flexible
- emotionally rich
- allows broad creative range

### Cons
- family relationship may be too subtle without strong visual branding

### Recommendation
Can work well if supported by a strong master brand and consistent design language.

---

## Master suite name shortlist

## 1. Timekeeper
### Why it works
- broad enough for collection, research, commerce, and time-travel experiences
- strong emotional undertone
- clear relationship to nostalgia and preservation
- memorable without being too literal

### Risks
- may sound slightly formal if branding is too stiff

### Verdict
**Best overall choice**

---

## 2. Memory Circuit
### Why it works
- strong fusion of nostalgia and technology
- feels cybernetic, emotional, and modular
- excellent fit for connected products

### Risks
- slightly more abstract
- may sound more niche or art-project-like

### Verdict
**Best alternative if we want something more evocative and less classical**

---

## 3. Afterglow
### Why it works
- emotional and atmospheric
- fits nostalgia and fading eras beautifully
- elegant and human

### Risks
- less obviously software-oriented
- may skew softer than desired for business/market tools

### Verdict
Strong poetic option

---

## 4. Ghost Signal
### Why it works
- retro-tech, mysterious, a little haunted
- excellent fit for archive and time-travel concepts

### Risks
- a bit darker and more niche
- may overfit the Reverse Matrix side versus the collection/dealer side

### Verdict
Excellent sub-brand territory

---

## 5. Neon Archive
### Why it works
- vivid visual identity
- archive and retro-future vibes in one phrase

### Risks
- slightly more aesthetic than foundational

### Verdict
Good but less timeless than Timekeeper

---

## Recommended master brand

### **Timekeeper**
Use this as the umbrella brand for the suite.

It gives us a clean framing:
- Timekeeper is the world
- the individual apps are the rooms

---

## App name system

### Flagship
- **RetroVault**

Keep the flagship name. It already has identity and momentum.

### Best current app candidates
- **TapeDeck** — movies and physical media
- **Reverse Matrix** — era-based archive explorer
- **Scanline** — research and archival reference
- **CartMarket** — market intelligence and dealer signals
- **Arcade** — presentation, showcase, public display
- **SaveState** — journaling and play memory
- **ShowRunner** — convention and event ops

---

### TapeDeck
Warm, tactile, media-first, era-rich.

### Reverse Matrix
Mythic, weird, ambitious, experiential.

### Scanline
Research, texture, visual fidelity, archival depth.

### CartMarket
Utility, strategy, velocity, dealer edge.

### Arcade
Display, wonder, public-facing delight.

### SaveState
Personal, reflective, emotionally sticky.

### ShowRunner
Energetic, practical, event-focused.

---

## Presentation strategy

User-facing naming can stay app-first:
- RetroVault
- TapeDeck
- Scanline
- Reverse Matrix

While formal or ecosystem-facing presentation can use the suite brand:
- A Timekeeper experience
- Part of the Timekeeper suite
- Powered by Timekeeper identity/search/context

That gives us both:
- strong individual app character
- clear ecosystem coherence

---

## Recommended path

### Master suite brand
**Timekeeper**

### Individual app names
Keep distinct names with strong personality.

### Internal rule
Each new app must sound like:
- a real place or machine
- not a SaaS dashboard
- emotionally evocative
- strong enough to stand alone

---

## Final recommendation

Use:
- **Timekeeper** as the umbrella suite brand
- **RetroVault** as the flagship collection core
- distinctive sibling names for each product

This gives the ecosystem range, coherence, and room to grow without feeling forced.
