# Retro Software Suite — Product Map

## Purpose

This document maps the likely products in the future retro-focused suite, their roles, their relationships, and a recommended order of development.

---

## Ecosystem framing

The suite is best understood as a set of connected products spanning six major domains:

1. collection
2. media
3. market
4. research
5. display
6. memory and time travel

Each product should stand alone.
Together, they should feel like a coherent ecosystem.

---

## Product catalog

## 1. RetroVault
### Role
Flagship collection core for retro games and related collecting workflows.

### Primary jobs
- track owned items, copies, condition, cost, and source
- support wishlist/watchlist flows
- power field buying and selling decisions
- surface collection analytics and sourcing insights

### Emotional character
Command center

### Strategic status
Already real. This is the anchor product.

---

## 2. TapeDeck
### Role
Physical media vault focused first on movies, then potentially broader collectible media.

### Primary jobs
- track VHS, LaserDisc, DVD, Blu-ray, box sets, editions, soundtracks, and related media
- support decade and genre exploration
- unify collection, discovery, and display for movies and adjacent formats

### Emotional character
Warm, tactile, cinematic

### Strategic status
Best first expansion after RetroVault

---

## 3. CartMarket
### Role
Dealer intelligence and market signal platform.

### Primary jobs
- surface pricing trends and comps
- identify arbitrage and sourcing opportunities
- guide what to hunt, move, or hold
- support dealer and reseller workflows

### Emotional character
Terminal, edge, hustle

### Strategic status
High-value business-adjacent expansion

---

## 4. Arcade
### Role
Public-facing presentation and display layer.

### Primary jobs
- collection showcase pages
- kiosk mode
- shelf and room presentation
- gallery-style views
- public collection storytelling

### Emotional character
Wonder, polish, spectacle

### Strategic status
Natural complement to RetroVault and TapeDeck

---

## 5. ShowRunner
### Role
Convention, expo, and market-event operations app.

### Primary jobs
- inventory packing and event prep
- booth planning
- trip logistics and budget tracking
- live sales and acquisition support
- event schedules and reminders

### Emotional character
Motion, preparation, execution

### Strategic status
Strong specialized companion for dealer workflows

---

## 6. Scanline
### Role
Research library and cultural context explorer.

### Primary jobs
- magazines
- manuals
- guides
- ads
- release history
- timelines
- supporting context around owned or sought-after items

### Emotional character
Reference terminal, signal archive

### Strategic status
Very strong ecosystem enrichment layer

---

## 7. SaveState
### Role
Personal memory and play journal.

### Primary jobs
- track what was played, watched, or revisited
- record milestones, notes, ratings, memories, and revisit queues
- preserve personal narrative around media

### Emotional character
Reflective, personal, intimate

### Strategic status
Adds heart and longitudinal memory to the suite

---

## 8. Reverse Matrix
### Role
Era-based digital archive explorer and digital time-travel product.

### Primary jobs
- let users explore preserved digital culture by era
- support web/BBS/terminal/gopher-style modes
- create authentic-feeling ritual rather than perfect completeness
- function as a standalone product with later ecosystem integration

### Emotional character
Mythic, haunting, exploratory

### Strategic status
Moonshot product, likely standalone first

---

## Shared object model (conceptual)

The ecosystem becomes much more powerful if core entities are shared conceptually across products.

Potential shared entities:
- title
- platform or format
- decade/year
- creator / studio / publisher / label
- genre
- condition / edition / release variant
- collection status
- tags
- person / place / event

Example:
A title like *Chrono Trigger* could simultaneously exist in:
- RetroVault as an owned game
- Scanline as a magazine/research subject
- SaveState as a played-and-remembered experience
- Arcade as a highlighted collection item
- Reverse Matrix as part of a 1995 digital culture context

This is where the suite becomes more than disconnected software.

## Recommended development order

## Phase 1 — strengthen the flagship and first adjacent expansion
1. RetroVault
2. TapeDeck
3. shared search and tagging concepts
4. shared design language

### Why
TapeDeck is closest to RetroVault in both architecture and market logic.
It lets the suite expand without immediately exploding into moonshot complexity.

---

## Phase 2 — add market and display power
5. CartMarket
6. Arcade
7. ShowRunner

### Why
These deepen practical value for collectors and dealers while increasing the ecosystem feel.

---

## Phase 3 — deepen memory, research, and digital time travel
8. Scanline
9. SaveState
10. Reverse Matrix

### Why
These are the most emotionally rich and ecosystem-defining products, but also the ones that benefit most from a strong base of shared objects, design patterns, and users.

---

## Strongest medium-term constellation

If the goal is to create the most compelling five-product suite after RetroVault, the strongest lineup is likely:

1. RetroVault
2. TapeDeck
3. CartMarket
4. Arcade
5. Reverse Matrix

This gives the ecosystem:
- ownership
- discovery
- commerce
- presentation
- time travel

That is a very distinctive strategic position.
